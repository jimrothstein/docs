02 MAR 2021


\textbf{State of Oregon v Zimmerman, Amy Lynn }

\textbf{18CR66984}

\textbf{4 OCT 2021 Hearing}

       Via FAX:  503.589.3266

Dear Honorable Judge Wren:


I am one of the listed victims in this case.   I last spoke before this Court
at arraignment ( 9 OCT 2018).   I wish to speak again, but due to Covid I hope
this letter will be acceptable.

According to the original DA, the purpose of prosecution was to effect "behavior
change," which I found then, and now, to be appropriate and necessary.

I have tried to follow this case via public court house records.    The VET
Court is not a transparent process.

Unfortunately, public records show little evidence of "behavior change."
Of course, this Court has more information than I do, but public records do
leave a clear written trail.

In those records there is abundance of litigation and disputes.   There are
patterns.  Inconsistencies that raise questions about accuracy of income and
financial issues.  The records detail the Defendant's considerable efforts,
spanning two states, to bring two or three additional children into her
household. Yet VET staff were unaware the State's child services had been
called to her residence.  I am also aware the Defendant is not employed, not
in school. 

Here are examples that I was able to verify myself:

*  The Defendant has continued to claim to be "hotel" when in
fact she has no short-term accommodations license.  The documentation is in
her own hand.    She knows the City of Salem  rules from prior litigation and
her own attempt to secure such a license.  

*   Last month the Defendant wrote to this Court. In fact, the Defendant has
 not made regular payments to the Court, has not done what she had agreed to.
 I double-checked this with Court's accounting office.  At one point
 Defendant's account for this case was deemed delinquent. Her account may
 *appear* in order, but that is because of sudden large cash payments.  The
 Defendant has made no payment in other criminal cases.


This is not behavior I would expect from someone who accepts responsibility and
no longer views herself as a 'victim'.[^1]

The DA has a longer list, and I would be happy to provide this to the Court.
Almost all of it is available in courthouse computer.  

We are here because of what the Defendant did and said inside another court
room.  Although that judge missed the felonies,  he did say
something very relevant here, echoed in writing by prior police/judges:  

>>  be sure to "dot every i and cross every t"

The probation calls for "zero tolerance".   But the Defendant is clever and
unafraid.  If you have not already done so, I hope you verify the facts for
yourself. 


Thank you for your time.
