9 JAN 2021

RE:  Litholink:  Ordering and Blood Collection.

Dear Dr. Bilbao:

Had a 30 minute telephone call with Litholink and this cleared up things that
might help streamline future orders.   Here is a summary of what they
suggested  and (below) additional instructions for lab technicians for the
blood draw.



***

For DOCTOR:

1.  Patient is responsible for getting the order to Litholink.

2.  Doctor is responsible for completing ONLY  medical portion of ordering
    form and giving it to patient.  Electronic version is perfectly OK.  The
    form itself is obtained from website (only registered providers have
    access to the form).  https://www.litholink.com/

3.  The patient should then review the order, complete his/her portion and
    send/fax/email it to Litholink.
    
***

For LAB TECHNICIAN:

Litholink is specialized lab for processing urine related to kidney stones.
A blood sample is very important to our analysis.

1.   Please review our instructions.   If you have any questions,  call
     Litholink (1-800-338-4333, 7 am - 7 pm, Chicago time) and we will clarify.

2.   Do the blood draw.

3.   Follow our directions for sample preparation (the Patient should wait in
     lobby).   DO NOT SEND WHOLE BLOOD.>

4.   Please use an SST tube if available to keep liquid separate from serum.

5.   Please put the date on the tube.

6.   Recall the patient and put the blood sample in the FedEx box the patient
     has.  Please point to the date and the serum separator, so Patient is
     aware.   

7.    The patient will bring the box to FedEx.   


THANK YOU VERY MUCH.
Questions?   Call Litholink:   1-800-338-4333
