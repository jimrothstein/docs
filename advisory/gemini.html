Recommended Infant Immunization Schedule: Birth to First Birthday

This document outlines the standard recommended vaccine schedule for infants from birth up to 12 months of age, based on clinical guidelines supported by major public health and medical organizations.

📅 Summary Schedule by Age

Birth

  • Hepatitis B (HepB): 1st dose

1 to 2 Months

  • Hepatitis B (HepB): 2nd dose

2 Months

  • DTaP (Diphtheria, Tetanus, acellular Pertussis): 1st dose
  • Hib (Haemophilus influenzae type b): 1st dose
  • IPV (Inactivated Poliovirus Vaccine): 1st dose
  • PCV (Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine): 1st dose
  • RV (Rotavirus): 1st dose

4 Months

  • DTaP: 2nd dose
  • Hib: 2nd dose
  • IPV: 2nd dose
  • PCV: 2nd dose
  • RV: 2nd dose

6 Months

  • DTaP: 3rd dose
  • Hib: 3rd dose (brand dependent)
  • PCV: 3rd dose
  • RV: 3rd dose (brand dependent)
  • Influenza (Flu): Annual vaccination begins (requires 2 doses 4 weeks apart for the first time)
  • COVID-19: Annual/seasonal vaccination series begins

6 to 12 Months

  • Hepatitis B (HepB): 3rd dose (typically given between 6 and 18 months)
  • IPV: 3rd dose (typically given between 6 and 18 months)

💉 Vaccine Breakdown and Target Diseases

Vaccine Acronym Full Vaccine Name Target Diseases Prevention
HepB Hepatitis B Vaccine Chronic liver infection, liver damage, liver cancer
DTaP Diphtheria, Tetanus, & acellular Pertussis Severe throat swelling, lockjaw, whooping cough
Hib Haemophilus influenzae type b Bacterial meningitis, pneumonia, epiglottitis
IPV Inactivated Poliovirus Vaccine Muscle paralysis, permanent disability, death
PCV Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine Bloodstream infections, meningitis, ear infections
RV Rotavirus Vaccine (Oral) Severe watery diarrhea, dehydration, vomiting
IIV / LAIV Influenza Vaccine Seasonal flu complications, severe pneumonia

Note: Combination vaccines (such as DTaP-HepB-IPV or DTaP-IPV-Hib-HepB) are frequently used by pediatricians to significantly reduce the overall number of physical injections an infant receives during a visit.

🛡️ Special / Seasonal Immunizations

  • RSV Monoclonal Antibody (Nirsevimab): Recommended as a single dose for newborns or young infants entering their first RSV season if the mother did not receive the RSV vaccine during pregnancy.
